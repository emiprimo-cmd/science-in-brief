Emiliano D. Primo, PhD

Microbial Biotechnology Scientist

Emiliano D. Primo is a microbiologist and biotechnology researcher specialized in microbial biotechnology, protein engineering, molecular biology, and applied R&D.

He began his academic training at the National University of Río Cuarto (UNRC), Argentina, where he earned his degree in Microbiology. He later completed a PhD in Biological Sciences at the same institution, combining research activities with university teaching in molecular biology, microbiology, and bioinformatics.

Following his doctoral studies, he continued his scientific career in Europe as a Postdoctoral Researcher at UCLouvain (Belgium) in the research group of Dr. Patrice Soumillion, where he worked on protein engineering, microbial biotechnology, enzyme development, and innovation-oriented research projects. He subsequently obtained competitive funding from the FNRS as Chargé de Recherches, supporting three additional years of postdoctoral research.

Throughout his career, he has combined academic research with applied innovation, including collaborations with industry, technology transfer, process optimization, and development of biotechnology solutions. This platform was created from his long-standing passion for science, technology, and continuous learning, with a strong habit of following scientific journals and translating new discoveries into clear, concise, and accessible insights for a broader audience.
